9.8.4 Compare Selections Area

Selecting an entry in the Deviation List or double-clicking a word (using the left mouse button while in the Select Mode or the right mouse button in any other mode) opens the Compare Selections Area. The Compare Selections Area is also activated by selecting one or more words and either choosing Compare Selections from the Edit menu or pressing Ctrl+D.

The selected words from both documents are displayed, and the word from the original has a blue background while the word from the copy has a red one. Blue character pairs indicate that they are the same, red character pairs indicate that they differ. The first pair of characters that is not matching (in red) automatically gets selected. The Unicode values of the characters and their description are displayed beneath the compared selection.

9.8.4.1 Braille Button

If the selected text includes numbers written in Braille, a Braille button appears at the top right of the Compare Selections Area. Hit the Braille button to see the numerical value of numbers written in Braille. Go back to the initial display by hitting the button again. For more information on the prerequisites for this translation, please see See Special Topics "Interpretation of Numbers in Braille" on page 1 Interpretation of Numbers in Braille.

9.8.4.2 Compare Selections Area for Corrections

When the entry in the Deviation List is from type Correction, the information displayed in the Compare Selection Areas is slightly different. By default, the Correction vs. Copy view is displayed and the text on the yellowish white background is the intended text from the original, whereas the text on the red background is the corresponding text from the copy. The intended text can be either the content of the correction itself or a combination of the correction’s applied instruction and the available original text.

It is possible to switch between different views by clicking on the button:

  • Original vs. Copy: the classical viewing option, as explained above.
  • Original vs. Correction: The text from the original is displayed against the content of the correction.

Once selected, the viewing option will be used for all Corrections, unless changed again.

Independent of the selected viewing option, a list of Associated corrections appears at the bottom of the Compare Selections Area. Each correction entry has information about its creator, type and time of creation.
