5.2.3 Critical Word Lists

The Critical Word List function includes predefined lists for

  • All numbers
    Check the context of each and every number.

  • Formatted numbers >= 1000 (European style e.g. 1.234.567,89)
    Double-check whether any formatting in the European style still exists, if you are aiming at US/UK style formatting.

  • Formatted numbers >= 1000 (US/UK e.g. 1,234,567.89)
    Double-check whether any formatting in the US/UK style still exists, if you are aiming at European style formatting.

  • Numbers with unit (e.g. “3 kg”, “7.5 °C” or “1%”)
    Check the context of any number followed by a unit.

  • Repeated words (e.g. “not not”)
    Check whether word repetitions are intended.

  • Trademarks (words followed by ©, ®, ™, etc.)
    Check whether the right words are followed by these symbols.

  • Words with missing trademark (©, ®, ™, etc.)
    Make sure that the items are followed by the corresponding upon each mentioning. Having it once with the correct symbol is sufficient.

In addition, you may define your own Critical Word List, for instance to include dosages and product names specific to your company. Customer specific Critical Word Lists usually include words of high importance which must always be identical in both the original manuscript and the copy. If you would like to create a special Critical Word List containing critical words for your company, you should contact your customer support partner.

The “Repeated Words” list is loaded by default prior to comparison. Further Critical Word Lists can be activated, either via the “Project” menu, or from the drop-down list of the “Prepare” tab in the Wizard toolbar. To deactivate a selected Critical Word List before comparison, click on it and the check mark next to it will disappear. To deactivate all lists at the same time, click on "Reset All".

After performing the comparison, the critical words can be viewed in the Deviation list by clicking on the CRIT button in the Status Bar at the bottom of the TVT window. When the CRIT button is selected, the specified critical words are all highlighted in light blue in both the original and the copy, thus enabling a faster and more focused verification.

Please note: The Critical Word List function only highlights words found in mapped text.
