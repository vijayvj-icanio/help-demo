24.2.1.1 Arabic-Indic Digit Category

This category serves dual purposes: First, some workflows for Arabic documents use European digits (0, 1, 2, …) in manuscripts, but replace them with Arabic-Indic digits (٠, ١, ٢ …) in the artwork. Second, the Unicode standard has two versions of Arabic-Indic digits (U+0660 to U+669, and U+06F0 to U+06F9), which appear identically for some digits, but slightly differently for others. Sometimes digits from both ranges are used interchangeably. Both situations are treated as warnings.
